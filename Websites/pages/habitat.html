- diamond (very strong bonds)

- graphite (very weak bonds, in pencils)

- fullerene and nanotube (in buckyballs) Anything that has carbon is an organic compound. Nonmetals may combine with carbon. Simplest organic compound is hydrocarbon (C and H only, ex. CH4)

- flammable, nonpolar, does not mix with H2O

- can form double bonds or triple bonds Substituted hydrocarbon: element other than C or H is attached to hydrocarbon Alcohols contain hydroxyl groups (-OH) Carboxylic acids are in foods (-COOH) Esters are fruity (-OCO)
